# Define additional time-entry productivity aids

Type: grilling
Status: resolved
Blocked by: [Define the time-entry interaction](04-define-the-time-entry-interaction.md)

Part of: [Specify a consulting time-entry MVP](../map.md)

## Question

Beyond the resolved weekly grid, persistent Client rows, direct duration entry, and spreadsheet keyboard movement, which additional speed features—such as copying a Time Entry, copying a prior week, filling several cells, or reusable description patterns—solve a necessary MVP workflow rather than adding avoidable complexity?

## Answer

The MVP adds no productivity aid beyond the interaction already resolved in [Define the time-entry interaction](04-define-the-time-entry-interaction.md): the weekly grid, persistent Client rows, direct descriptionless Billable duration entry, and spreadsheet keyboard movement are the complete fast path.

Copying a Time Entry, copying a prior week, filling multiple cells, and reusable description patterns are deliberately omitted. No necessary recurring workflow currently justifies their complexity or the risk of reproducing stale dates, durations, billability classifications, descriptions, or locked historical composition. These aids should be reconsidered only when observed use identifies a concrete repetitive workflow; they are not latent MVP requirements.
